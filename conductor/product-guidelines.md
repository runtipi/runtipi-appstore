# Product Guidelines

## Content & Tone
### Friendly & Helpful Prose
The tone for app descriptions and project documentation should be approachable and easy for beginners to understand. While technical accuracy is essential, avoid overly dense jargon when a simpler explanation suffices. Use an encouraging voice to help users confidently set up and use their self-hosted applications.

### Plain Language Documentation
All documentation must be written in plain, accessible English. Break down complex processes into manageable steps and use clear, direct language to ensure users from all backgrounds can follow along.

## UX & Branding
### Visual Consistency
To maintain a professional and cohesive store appearance, all app logos and visual assets must follow a standardized format. Ensure that logos are clear and properly scaled for the Runtipi UI.

### Standardized Templates
Every app entry in the store must use the standard template for its metadata and descriptions. This consistency allows users to quickly find and compare information across different applications.

### Trusted Linking
All external links—including links to upstream source code, documentation, and official websites—must point to official or well-vetted, trusted sources. This ensures the security and reliability of the information provided to users.

## Accessibility
### Descriptive Alt Text
All images, including app logos and screenshots, must include descriptive alternative text (alt text). This ensures that users relying on screen readers can understand the visual content of the App Store.
